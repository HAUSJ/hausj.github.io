Overview

IKEA Place

IKEA Place is an augmented reality furnishing app that gives users an accurate impression of the furniture’s size, design and functionality in their home. With this app, users could make better and faster decisions when purchasing furniture in IKEA.

PROBLEM AND OPPORTUNITY

Through guerrilla usability testing, I found that under the high learning curve of augmented reality, users’ limited control of the furniture’s spatial position and tedious user flow to change furnitures could discourage users from using the app and potentially from purchasing IKEA’s furniture.

SOLUTION

I redesign and simply the users flow (from scanning the room, loading furnitures, controlling furnitures to replacing furnitures) to provide users with intuitive controls and flexibility, and boost users’ confidence when using AR technology to find their favorite furnitures.

Guerrilla Usability Testing

To uncover pain points while using the ios app, I conducted 5 guerrilla usability tests in coffee shops in San Francisco. The selected users are reasonably close to the target market of IKEA and among which 2 users have previous experience with AR technology.

I gave each user three scenario based tasks to complete within the app. Senario example is like:"You are planing to buy a bed, a nightstand and a desk for the new bedroom which you just moved into. Open up the Ikea Place app in your phone, try to find the furnitures you like and put/arrange them in your room."


Insights from the testing were clustered to identify relationships and reveal main frustration users had when using the app. These clusters were mapped in diagram to evaluate their importance for users and business, which also determined priorities to move forward.


Task Flow

Base on the study, here are the 3 main problems I decided to focus on:

1.Furniture Loading

  • Users are forced to wait the scanning process to be finished and could not either exit or ask for help.
  • Furnitures pop up randomly on the ground after the scan process. Users sometimes could not find the furnitures.

2.Furniture Positioning

  • Users find it not precise to place the furniture to the position they want with finger swiping on the screen.

3.Furniture Replacing

  • Users could not try other colors and alternatives of the furniture after placing it and find it tedious to have to delete the previous one and go through the process again.
